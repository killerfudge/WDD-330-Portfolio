    We should design apps for the least powerful devices to ensure we focus on the fundamental parts of our design.

    We need to be aware of all the different kinds and forms of devices that our programs will appear on.

    Start small and scale up

    People don’t always know what they want until it is given to them.

    Make the app’s purpose as obvious as possible as soon as possible.

    The more steps it takes to complete an action, the more frustration the user is likely to build.

    Keep in mind how people hold their device and design the product to have important interactions where it will be easiest to reach.

    Don’t assume users will recognize icons just because they are used consistently.

    When dealing with large forms, it’s a good idea to split the forms into several pages with a progress indicator. Utilize autofill

    Find even small ways to simplify what the user needs to do with the program.
